Amphibians

AFRICAN BULLFROG
This large frog can swell even larger to try to scare away enemies. It makes a loud sound, and in dry periods it will live in the ground until it rains

ALPINE NEWT
The male alpine newt is known for its very colorful patterns for attracting the female. Its tails is flattened like a fin to help it swim.

EMPEROR NEWT
Newts are closely related to salamanders and tend to be found in or near water. The emperor newt is also called the alligator newt because of the ridge of bumps on its back.

CHILEAN RED-SPOTTED TOAD
At only 1 inch (2.5 cm) long, this tiny toad has learned to live in the very high Andes Mountains (13,000 ft/4,000 m).

AXOLOTL
This Mexican Salamander spends all of its life in the water. Like a fish, it breathes through gills near its neck.

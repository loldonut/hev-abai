MAMMALS

ANTEATER
This Latin American animal has no teeth, but with its long head, an even longer tongue, and a nose that smells 40 times better than ours, it is well-equipped to find its favorite food: ants, and termites.

YAK
The hairy yak is related to the cow and is found in the high mountains of Asia. It is an important source of food, wool and hides.

BEAR
All bears, except for the white polar bear, eat plants as well as animals. In colder regions, some bears will sleep in caves or dens for most of the winter.

TIGER
Here is the biggest of the wild cats - the only one known to attack humans for food! Its striped coat allows it to hide easily in the jungle. Watch out!

RACCOON
The raccoon is known for its ringed tail, and it looks like it is wearing a mask! It also washes its food in water before it eats.

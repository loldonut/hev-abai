REPTILES

PRICKLENAPE AGAMA
Like the chameleon, agama lizards are in the iguana family. The pricklenape, or 'spiky-necked,' agama has long legs and toes for moving quickly in trees.

CHAMELEON
These amazing lizards can change colors to show their feelings or to hide. The chameleon's tongue is as long as its body and can catch an insect in one-hundreth of a second.

PYTHON
The python family includes the biggest longest and strongest snakes in the world (up to 33 feet/10 m long). These snakes are not poisonous, instead they kill their prey by squeezing slowly.

FRILLED LIZARD
This Australian lizard has a fan of skin on its neck to make it look larger than it really is. It will also stand up on its back legs to scare away attackers or other competing lizards.

LEATHERBACK TURTLE
This is the largest of all turtles (one weighed 1,900lb/860 kg!). It is a sea turtle and can swim underwater for three-quaters of a mile (1.2 km) before coming up for air.

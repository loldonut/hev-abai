BIRDS

HUMMINGBIRD
These tiny birds live only in the Americas and some grow to just 2.5 in. (6.4 cm) in length! They hover in mid air as they drink nectar from flowers

FLAMINGO
This pink water bird has a special crooked beak for filtering its food. It builds a nest of mud and only lays one egg at a time.

PEACOCK
The male peacock has an amazing fan of colorful feathers. It opens this 'train' of feathers to attract the plain brown female

PARROT
Parrots use their short, strong bills to crack open nuts and seeds. Some parrots squawk loudly and can even be taught to say a few words.

OSTRICH
This is the biggest bird in the world (up to 9 feet/2.75 m tall). Although it can't fly, it can run very fast on its long, strong legs.
